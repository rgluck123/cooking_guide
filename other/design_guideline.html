Efficient Mobile Cooking Guide

Interface Design Guideline

A complete spec for vibe-coding the app — colors, typography, sizing, components, icons, and imagery.

1. Design vibe

The app feels like a warm, organized kitchen notebook — natural, calm, and trustworthy. It is not clinical or cold. The mood is fresh ingredients on a wooden counter: soft greens, warm creams, and golden accents. Food is always the visual hero. The UI stays out of the way — generous whitespace, rounded shapes, and gentle shadows let photography breathe. Interactions feel smooth and unhurried, with subtle feedback that rewards the user without demanding attention.

Usage rules

• Page background: #F2F4EF — never white for the outer canvas.
• Cards and sheets: #FFFFFF with 1px solid #D6D3C8 border.
• Primary action button: #2E2E2E fill, white label — not green.
• Green accent only on CTAs that are secondary or confirmatory (e.g. "Start Cooking").
• Never use pure black (#000) or pure white (#fff) for text or backgrounds.

3. Typography

Two fonts only. Playfair Display (serif) for headings — creates warmth and appetite. Inter (sans-serif) for all body, labels, and UI text — clean and legible at small sizes.

7. Imagery style

Tone: Warm, natural, well-lit — daylight or golden hour. Avoid studio-cold white backgrounds.
Color grading: Warm highlights (+10 temp), slightly lifted shadows. Greens feel fresh, not neon.
Composition: Overhead (flat-lay) or 45° angle. Hero images fill the card edge-to-edge. Never portrait crop on landscape cards.
Subjects: Real ingredients, texture-forward. Show the cooking process, not just the plated result.
Aspect ratios: 16:9 for hero banners · 4:3 for recipe cards · 1:1 for cuisine category tiles · 3:4 for step-by-step instruction photos.
Overlay text: Use a 20–30% dark gradient scrim at the bottom if placing text on top of images. Never a solid block.
